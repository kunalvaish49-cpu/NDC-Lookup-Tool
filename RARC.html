        NDC Verification
        💊
        Use the NDC Drug Lookup Tool to Verify
        Search the NDC billed on the claim using the tool below. Compare the billed NDC against the correct NDC for the procedure/J-code before deciding.

        Does the NDC billed on the claim match the correct NDC?

        NDC Matched — Coding Review Required
        💡
        Important Note on Matched NDCs
        Whenever the NDC code appears correct on the lookup, the issue is typically that the Billing NDC Code was selected incorrectly. NDCs must always be selected as per the prescription. Please check the Medical Records under Treatment Notes.
        Action Steps
        1. 1
          Check Medical Records — Review Treatment Notes to confirm the NDC matches the drug documented in the original prescription.
        2. 2
          Assign to Coding Team for further action and verified NDC confirmation.
        3. 3
          If the Coding Team verifies a Correct NDC — Update the NDC in your Practice Management System (PMS).
        4. 4
          Resubmit the Corrected Claim to insurance with the verified NDC in proper 11-digit format.
        NDC Not Matched — Verification Required
        ❌
        NDC Mismatch Identified
        The NDC billed does not correspond to the correct NDC for the procedure/J-code. Immediate prescription verification is required before any resubmission or call.
        Action Steps
        1. 1
          Verify with the Original Prescription of the Drug — Pull the prescription and confirm the correct NDC for the drug actually administered.
        2. 2
          Send to Coding Team for Further Action — Coding team will verify the correct NDC and advise on the corrected claim.
        Call Insurance — Step-by-Step Script
        ✅
        NDC Verified by Coding Team — Ready to Call
        Follow the script below carefully. Fill in the Claim Details panel on the right as you go through the call.
        Step 1 — Initial Request for Reprocessing
        "Hi, I am calling regarding Claim # [CLAIM NUMBER] for patient [PATIENT NAME], DOS [DATE OF SERVICE]. The claim was denied with RARC N846/N816 — NDC issue. We have verified the correct NDC is [NDC NUMBER]. Can you please send this claim back for reprocessing?"
        Step 2 — If Rep Denies Reprocessing
        1. A
          Ask the Rep: "Are you able to provide the correct NDC number that the payer has on file for this J-code?"
        2. B
          If Rep provides NDC → Note the NDC, update in PMS, and resubmit the corrected claim.
        3. C
          If Rep cannot provide NDC → Proceed to the Appeal / Reconsideration process in the next step.
        Step 3 — Ask About Reconsideration / Dispute Process
        "Does your plan have a Reconsideration or Dispute process for this type of denial?"
        1. A
          If Yes → Ask: "Do you have a Dispute or Reconsideration Letter template we need to submit with our request?"
        2. B
          If No Reconsideration/Dispute process exists → Inform the team and proceed with submitting a formal Appeal.
        Step 4 — Dispute / Reconsideration Letter Details
        1. A
          If Rep says Yes they have a letter → Ask: "Can you provide the website URL or your fax number for submission?"
        2. B
          Ask: "What documents should be attached with the Reconsideration submission?"
        3. C
          ⚠️ Always attach the Denied EOB with any appeal or reconsideration — no exceptions.
        Step 5 — Confirm Timely Filing & TAT
        "What is the timely filing limit for submitting this appeal or reconsideration?" "What is the expected turnaround time if the claim is sent back for reprocessing?"
        📌
        Document TAT, Rep Name, and Call Reference # in the Claim Details panel on the right before ending the call.
